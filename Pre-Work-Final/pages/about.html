I Love Music

Growing up in Jersey City I attended the local public school in downtown. A lot of my close friends were in the school band and would usually get excused out of class for band practice and go on trips to recitals across the state. What initial started out as a plot to ditch class out of boredom, turned into a life-long love and respect for music as a discipline, creative medium and means of expression. I joined in the 5th grade and went on to playing in a small jazz ensemble for the next 3 years till graduation as a part of the NJCU Performing Arts Program. Though these days I don't play much clarinet, I still play guitar as often as I can.

Practice Playing Guitar

How I ended up learning to play guitar is a bit of a funny story. I was on my way home from a friends house and had seen a discarded guitar in the trash in front of a funeral home. It was in horrible condition, it had holes in the body and the strings were old and corroded. Despite all its flaws, I took it home, clipped the strings, cleaned it up, and watched a youtube video on how to restring acoustic guitars. The following day I purcharsed a beginners music book for guitars and the supplies I needed from the local music shop and got to work doing it myself. Fortunate for me I had already known how to read sheet music and with my prior experience in the band, tuning and using my ear to discern sound, I was able to know if I was able to produce the sound I needed to practice effieciently.
